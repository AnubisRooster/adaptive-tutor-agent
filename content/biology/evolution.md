# Evolution

Evolution is the change in the heritable characteristics of populations over generations. It is the unifying theme of biology: "nothing in biology makes sense except in the light of evolution" (Dobzhansky).

## Natural Selection

Darwin's core mechanism rests on a few observations:

1. Organisms produce more offspring than can survive.
2. Individuals **vary**, and some variation is heritable.
3. Some variants survive and reproduce better in their environment.
4. Therefore advantageous heritable traits become more common over generations.

The key phrase is **differential reproductive success** — "fitness" means reproductive success, not strength. Selection acts on phenotypes, but only heritable variation evolves.

## Sources of Variation

Evolution needs raw material:
- **Mutation** introduces new alleles (the ultimate source of variation).
- **Recombination** during meiosis reshuffles existing alleles.
- **Gene flow** (migration) moves alleles between populations.

## Other Mechanisms

Natural selection isn't the only force:
- **Genetic drift**: random changes in allele frequency, strong in small populations.
- **Sexual selection**: traits that improve mating success (e.g., a peacock's tail) can spread even if they hurt survival.
- **Founder effects and bottlenecks**: chance events that shrink genetic diversity.

## Speciation

A **species** is often defined as populations that can interbreed and produce fertile offspring. When populations become reproductively isolated — geographically or otherwise — they accumulate differences until they can no longer interbreed. That split is **speciation**.

## Evidence

Evolution is supported by converging lines of evidence: the fossil record, shared anatomy (homologous structures), embryology, biogeography, and especially molecular data — DNA similarities map neatly onto the tree of life. Observed examples include antibiotic resistance and pesticide resistance evolving in real time.

## Common Misconceptions

Evolution is not "just a theory" in the casual sense; a scientific theory is a well-supported explanation. Organisms don't evolve traits "on purpose," and individuals don't evolve — **populations do**, across generations.

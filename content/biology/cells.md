# Cell Biology

The **cell** is the basic unit of life: the smallest thing that is unambiguously alive. All organisms are made of one or more cells, all cells come from pre-existing cells, and the cell is where life's chemistry happens.

## Prokaryotes vs. Eukaryotes

- **Prokaryotes** (bacteria, archaea): small, no membrane-bound nucleus; DNA floats in the cytoplasm. Simpler and ancient.
- **Eukaryotes** (plants, animals, fungi, protists): larger, with a true **nucleus** and membrane-bound **organelles** that compartmentalize functions.

## Key Organelles (Eukaryotic)

- **Nucleus**: stores DNA and controls gene expression.
- **Mitochondria**: produce ATP via cellular respiration — the "powerhouses." They have their own DNA, hinting at an ancient bacterial origin (endosymbiosis).
- **Ribosomes**: build proteins from mRNA.
- **Endoplasmic reticulum**: rough ER (with ribosomes) makes proteins; smooth ER makes lipids.
- **Golgi apparatus**: modifies, sorts, and ships proteins.
- **Chloroplasts** (plants): perform photosynthesis.
- **Lysosomes**: digest waste and recycle components.

## The Cell Membrane

The plasma membrane is a **phospholipid bilayer**: phosphate "heads" face water; fatty-acid "tails" face inward, away from water. This makes it **selectively permeable** — small nonpolar molecules pass freely, while ions and large polar molecules need protein channels or transporters. Embedded proteins handle transport, signaling, and recognition. This "fluid mosaic" structure is a perfect example of **structure determining function**.

## Transport

- **Passive transport** (no energy): diffusion and osmosis move substances down their concentration gradient.
- **Active transport** (uses ATP): pumps move substances against their gradient, like the sodium-potassium pump that maintains nerve and muscle function.

## Why It Matters

Almost every topic in biology — energy, genetics, physiology, disease — ultimately plays out inside or between cells. Understanding membranes, organelles, and transport gives you the vocabulary for all of them.
